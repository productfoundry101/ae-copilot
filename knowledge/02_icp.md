# Personio ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

*Internal · Sales Enablement · Q1 2026*

The ICP is who we sell to best — where the product fit is strongest, where we win most often, and where retention and expansion are highest. It's not who we *only* sell to.

If a deal is well outside ICP, that doesn't mean walk away — but it does mean apply more scepticism.

---

## Sweet spot

| Dimension | Sweet spot | Edges |
|---|---|---|
| **Employees** | 200 – 2,000 | 100 – 5,000 OK; <100 too small; >5,000 needs ENT engagement |
| **Region** | DACH, UKI, Nordics, Benelux, Iberia, France | Other European OK; non-European: out |
| **Industry** | Retail, Hospitality, Logistics, Professional Services, Manufacturing, Tech | Heavy regulated (finance, defence) needs extra scrutiny |
| **Tech maturity** | Currently on legacy HRIS, spreadsheets, or split tools | Already on Workday/SAP SuccessFactors: harder displacement |
| **HR team size** | 3 – 20 dedicated HR staff | <3 = capacity issue for adoption; >20 often have enterprise needs we don't cover |
| **Growth stage** | Stable-growth or scaling (15–40% YoY) | Hypergrowth fine; static or shrinking: pull renewal forward |

## Buying triggers we look for

These are signals that *now* is the moment, not just that they fit:

- New CHRO or VP People in role (first 6 months)
- Recent rapid headcount growth (>20% in 12 months)
- Multi-country expansion (especially first international hire)
- Replacing a legacy system (often Sage, SAP HR on-prem, ADP local, spreadsheets)
- Compliance trigger (GDPR audit, new payroll regulation, works council push)
- M&A — acquired company on different HR stack
- IPO prep / fundraise — need professional HR reporting

If none of these are present, it's a "we should look at HR systems at some point" deal. Those slip. A lot.

---

## Anti-patterns (deals to deprioritise)

- **<100 employees** — usually price-sensitive, low ACV, low expansion. Refer to partner channel.
- **>5,000 employees with global complexity** — we don't have multi-entity payroll across all regions yet. Don't promise what we can't deliver.
- **Already happy on Workday or SAP SuccessFactors** — displacement cost too high without a specific catalyst.
- **No named HR leader** — buying committee is unclear, decision will stall.
- **Single-country US-only** — not our market.

## Persona briefs

### CHRO / VP People (Economic Buyer)
**Cares about:** strategic HR transformation, business outcomes (retention, productivity, time-to-hire), being seen as modernising. Usually came up through generalist HR or PeopleOps.

**Talks like:** "people strategy," "employee experience," "data-driven HR."

**Won by:** roadmap fit with their strategy, exec peer references, business-case framing not feature lists. Lost by feature checklists and vendor-driven demos.

### Head of People Operations (Champion or EB-lite)
**Cares about:** day-to-day process efficiency, reducing HR ticket volume, freeing the team for strategic work. Has the most pain, usually the strongest champion.

**Talks like:** specific processes (onboarding, payroll prep, performance cycles), tools they currently fight.

**Won by:** showing the *workflow*, not the feature. Time-saved specifics. A demo that mirrors their actual process. Lost by generic demos.

### CFO / Head of Finance (Influencer or co-EB on larger deals)
**Cares about:** payroll accuracy, audit-ready records, total cost (Personio vs. status quo), implementation risk.

**Talks like:** "ROI," "TCO," "implementation timeline," "what's the worst-case scenario."

**Won by:** concrete ROI math, references on payroll accuracy, transparent pricing. Lost by hand-wavy benefits.

### Head of IT (Technical buyer)
**Cares about:** SSO/SAML, integrations (especially with finance ERP and time tracking), data security, GDPR posture, implementation effort.

**Talks like:** "integration architecture," "data residency," "SSO," "API," "vendor lock-in."

**Won by:** clear technical answers, no marketing fluff. Lost by vague responses to technical questions.

### HRBP / HR Manager (User)
**Cares about:** the day-to-day. Will the new system actually be easier? Does it match how they work now?

**Talks like:** specific scenarios — "what happens when a manager wants to give a one-off bonus?"

**Won by:** showing real workflows. Hands-on time in the demo. Lost by speaking only to leadership.

## A note on industry

Retail and hospitality are over-represented in our customer base because shift work + high turnover = high HR overhead. We have strong customer references in both. If you're working a retail or hospitality deal, lean into this.

Logistics and manufacturing are growing fast. Strong fit, fewer references — flag if you need one and we'll find it.

Professional services is a steady performer. Often smaller, often founder-led; CEO can be involved in the buying decision.

Tech is split — the well-funded ones buy us, the bootstrapped ones build their own. The signal is whether they have a Head of People (buy) vs. a part-time founder-doing-HR (build).
