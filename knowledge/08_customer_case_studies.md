# Customer case studies

*For AE reference · use as proof points in discovery and demo*

Short, specific, named. The best reference call is one your prospect can recognise themselves in — match on industry, size, and pain shape.

If you want to set up a reference call, ping #customer-references with the account + persona you're trying to match.

## Lumen Retail Group (DACH, Retail, 1,200 employees)

**Background.** Lumen runs 180 stores across Germany and Austria. Previously on a SAP HR on-prem instance set up in 2014, plus three different payroll providers. Triggered by a board mandate to consolidate HR systems following a 2024 acquisition of a 400-person hospitality chain.

**Why they chose Personio.** Native DACH payroll, modern UI that part-time store staff could actually use, faster implementation timeline than Workday's bid (10 weeks vs. 9 months quoted), and an exec sponsor who had used Personio at a previous company.

**Outcome.** Live in 9 weeks. Payroll runs cleanly across both entities. HR ops team reports ~40% reduction in time spent on payroll prep. Net Promoter from employees went from 22 to 41 in the first year.

**Use as a reference for:** retail customers, DACH multi-entity, post-acquisition consolidation, Workday displacement.

## Sable Hospitality (Iberia, Hospitality, 650 employees)

**Background.** Hotel group with 14 properties across Spain and Portugal. Heavy shift-work HR overhead — over 65% of staff are hourly. Previously used a custom-built tool plus paper-based onboarding.

**Why they chose Personio.** Time tracking integrated with payroll, Spanish and Portuguese payroll native, ability for property-level managers to handle their own staffing without funnelling through head office.

**Outcome.** Live in 8 weeks. Onboarding time dropped from 14 days to 3. The Head of People runs a 4-person team where before she ran 7 — the additional 3 were absorbed into customer-facing roles. Saved approximately €280k in annualised HR ops cost.

**Use as a reference for:** hospitality, hourly workforce, Iberia, mid-market hospitality with multi-location complexity.

## Cascade Logistics (Nordics, Logistics, 1,800 employees)

**Background.** Pan-Nordic logistics player. Heavy union involvement, complex collective bargaining agreements, four-country payroll. Previously on a patchwork of country-specific tools.

**Why they chose Personio.** Multi-country payroll under one roof (Norway, Sweden, Denmark, Finland), strong handling of works council requirements, ability to model union-specific pay rules.

**Outcome.** Live in 14 weeks (longer than typical due to union approval cycles). Eliminated three legacy vendors. Compliance reporting now takes a single afternoon instead of three days per cycle. Successfully passed a Norwegian works council audit six months post-deployment.

**Use as a reference for:** logistics, Nordics, multi-country payroll, union-heavy environments, works council scrutiny.

## Vela Software (UK, Tech, 320 employees)

**Background.** B2B SaaS company. Hired aggressively post-Series-B (140 → 320 in 18 months). Previously on HiBob; outgrew the payroll integration as headcount grew across UK + Ireland + Spain.

**Why they chose Personio.** Native payroll across all three countries, recruiting integrated for high-volume hiring, performance management module ready for their first formal review cycle.

**Outcome.** Live in 7 weeks. Onboarding flow now scales with hiring volume without adding HR ops headcount. Performance review participation went from 65% to 92% in the first cycle on Personio (vs. last cycle on Bob).

**Use as a reference for:** scaling tech companies, HiBob displacement, multi-country expansion.

## Lost deal: Petrichor Industries (DACH, Manufacturing, 2,800 employees)

We don't typically share these externally, but the AE team can use the pattern in internal conversations.

**Background.** German industrial manufacturer. We were one of three vendors evaluated alongside Workday and a niche manufacturing-specific HRIS.

**Why we lost.** They had Workday already deployed in finance, and the CFO led the selection. We were never going to win that one given the bundle pressure and incumbent advantage. The deal also had no clear catalyst — they were evaluating because it was "due time," not because anything was broken.

**Lessons.**
- Workday-already-in-finance + CFO leading = qualify carefully before investing.
- No buying trigger means slow process and incumbent advantage. Trigger > stated interest.
- Champion was Head of People, but she didn't have political weight against the CFO. Multi-thread to Finance leadership earlier.

## How we use these in deals

- In discovery, surface 1–2 references that match the prospect's shape *before* they ask. ("There's a logistics customer of ours — Cascade — who had a very similar union challenge. Want me to set up a call?")
- In demos, reference the case study at the moment that's relevant to the slide. Specifics beat generalities.
- In proposals, include 2–3 brief reference quotes inline, with permission. The reference team has these on file.

Reference quotas: each customer agrees to a fixed number of reference calls per year. Don't burn references on low-probability deals.
