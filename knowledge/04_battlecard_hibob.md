# Battlecard: Personio vs HiBob

*Internal · Last updated: 2026-04*

HiBob is our closest direct competitor in European mid-market. We see them most in tech-flavoured SMB/lower-MM deals (100–700 employees), particularly in UK and Israel-anchored buyers.

---

## Headline positioning

> HiBob optimises for the *culture* layer of HR. Personio optimises for the *operating* layer. We win when customers need HR operations that actually run — payroll, compliance, performance, recruiting — not just engagement surveys and an org chart that looks good.

---

## When we win

- Customer has real payroll complexity (multi-country, multi-entity, hourly + salaried)
- Customer cares about compliance and audit trail (especially GDPR + works council + country-specific labour law)
- Customer needs recruiting integrated, not bolted on
- Customer values implementation discipline and account team continuity
- CFO or COO is involved in the decision (we're more credible as an operations system)
- Customer has tried HiBob and outgrown the operations side

## When we lose

- Smaller SMB (100–300) where UI and culture features matter more than ops depth
- Customer is "Bob-first" culturally — UK tech founders, often
- Decision led by an HR leader who already has Bob loyalty
- Demo-driven decision (HiBob demos *very* well)
- Cost-sensitive deal at the lower end of our ICP

---

## Their pitch (what they'll say)

- "Beautiful UX, employees love it" → true, design is genuinely good
- "Quick to set up" → true, faster than Workday, comparable to Personio
- "Strong on engagement and culture features" → true, where they invest most
- "Modern company, modern HR" → marketing line
- "Talent management built in" → reasonable, varies by module

## Our counter (what to say back)

- We're the system HR operations actually run on. Payroll, compliance, audit, recruiting — first-class, not afterthoughts.
- European-first: native payroll across DACH, Iberia, France, Nordics. Bob partners or integrates for payroll in most markets — that's a different operational model.
- Implementation discipline: we have a methodology, you'll have an implementation manager, you'll go live on the date we agreed.
- Recruiting + HRIS in one system, not two systems integrated. Important when the recruiting pipeline feeds onboarding directly.
- We're the platform you grow into. Customers don't typically outgrow Personio in the 200–2,000 employee range. They do outgrow Bob.

---

## Talk tracks for specific objections

**"Our employees love how Bob looks"**
Fair, the UI is good. So is ours — worth a side-by-side in the demo. But: employees use HRIS a few times a year. Your HR team uses it daily. Optimise for who actually has to make it work.

**"Bob is faster to implement"**
We're typically similar timeline (6–10 weeks). The difference is what's *running* at the end of implementation — with us, payroll, compliance, recruiting are all live. With Bob, payroll is usually a separate vendor integration on top, which is where timelines slip.

**"We don't need all the operations stuff"**
Today, maybe not. In 18 months, when you're 1,400 people across three countries with a payroll error costing you €40k in goodwill, you will. Worth talking to a few customers about what changed for them at growth inflection points.

**"Bob is cheaper"**
Sometimes. Worth pricing the full picture: HRIS + payroll + recruiting + integrations + implementation. We usually come out within 10% — and ahead on TCO once payroll is in scope. Want to put real numbers on this?

---

## Discovery questions

- "Are you also evaluating HiBob? What's drawing you to them?"
- "What's the payroll situation today? In-house, outsourced, multi-country?"
- "What's the recruiting motion? Volume, geography, how many tools?"
- "Who in the company will use this daily? Weekly? Monthly?"
- "What's your view on having HRIS and payroll on separate systems?"

---

## Note on positioning

Don't trash HiBob. We respect them, they're a good product. We win on operational depth and European fit, they win on culture-layer features and SMB design. Saying "Bob is bad" makes us look insecure. Saying "we're the system that runs your business, not the system that surveys your employees" lands.
