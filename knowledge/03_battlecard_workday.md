# Battlecard: Personio vs Workday

*Internal · Last updated: 2026-04*

We see Workday in deals at the upper end of our ICP (1,500+ employees) and in enterprise displacement situations.

---

## Headline positioning

> Workday is built for the Global 2000. Personio is built for European mid-market and growing companies. We're not trying to be Workday for 50,000-person enterprises — we're trying to be the HR operating system European companies actually want.

---

## When we win

- Customer is European mid-market (200 – 2,000 employees) and pragmatic about TCO
- Implementation time matters (we deploy in weeks, Workday in many months)
- HR team is small and needs an opinionated, fast tool — not a configuration project
- Legal / compliance is European-first (GDPR, works council, country-specific payroll)
- Customer values modern UI / employee experience
- Buyer has been burned by an over-scoped enterprise rollout before

## When we lose

- Customer is genuinely enterprise (5,000+) and needs Workday's depth in finance + HCM
- Workday is already in for finance — bundle pressure
- Customer's selection committee includes a "Workday person" with prior loyalty
- Procurement-led RFP with no real evaluation criteria — Workday's enterprise brand wins
- Multi-region / multi-entity payroll across 10+ countries

---

## Their pitch (what they'll say)

- "One platform for HR + Finance + Planning" → true at scale, more than most mid-market needs
- "Used by the Fortune 500" → true, also expensive and slow to deploy
- "Configurable for any process" → true, also: months of consulting to configure it
- "Continuous innovation, twice-yearly releases" → true, but mid-market doesn't need 80% of new features
- "Best-in-class analytics" → reasonable, depends on the comparison

## Our counter (what to say back)

- We're built for *European* mid-market — country-specific payroll is native, not a module
- Time-to-value: customers live in 6–10 weeks, not 6–18 months
- HR ops team doesn't need a Workday consultant on retainer to use us
- Modern UI: people actually open the app instead of avoiding it
- Total cost: license + implementation + ongoing config support, all-in, we're typically 50–70% lower for sub-2,000 employees

---

## Talk tracks for specific objections

**"We need one system across HR and Finance"**
Fair point at enterprise scale. For most mid-market, we integrate cleanly with the finance system you already have. Forcing one platform tends to mean compromising on either HR or finance — usually HR. What's the actual cost of the integration you have today?

**"Workday is the standard"**
Standard for whom? At 50,000 employees, yes. At 800, you're paying for capability you'll never use, and adding 6 months to your timeline. Standard isn't free.

**"We want best-in-class analytics"**
Workday's reporting is powerful and complex. Personio reporting covers 90% of mid-market needs out of the box, with the option to export to your BI tool for the rest. What's the actual reporting use case driving this?

**"We're already in evaluation with them"**
Understandable — they're a sensible reference point. Worth a separate conversation specifically about TCO over 3 years including implementation. We can put numbers in front of you that often surprise people.

---

## Discovery questions to ask early

If you suspect Workday is the competitor (or the incumbent in finance):

- "Are you also evaluating Workday? Where are you in that process?"
- "If Workday is already in for finance, what's the rationale for keeping HR separate / on a different platform?"
- "What's your honest sense of timeline tolerance? When do you need this live?"
- "Who's championing Workday internally? Have they used it before?"

---

## Red flag combinations (consider qualifying out)

- Workday already deployed in finance AND CFO leads the evaluation AND timeline is flexible
- 4,000+ employees, multi-region, IPO-track
- Procurement-led RFP with Workday as named requirement

If two of those are true, the deal is uphill. If all three, walk.
